What is Artificial Intelligence?
Computers and robots have the remarkable ability to learn,
reason, and make decisions in ways that closely resemble
human thought processes. For example, artificial intelligence is
embedded in many everyday devices, such as smartphones,
which can comprehend and respond to spoken commands.
When you interact with a voice assistant, it listens to your spo-
ken words, analyzes them, and provides you with relevant an-
swers or actions. The practical applications of AI are vast and
varied. Speech recognition technology, for instance, empowers
computers to understand and interpret human speech effec-
tively. This technology is utilized in dictation software that
transforms your spoken words into written text, making com-
munication more efficient. Additionally, AI demonstrates
exceptional proficiency in decision-making; for example,
numerous online services analyze your previous purchases and
preferences to suggest products that you may find interesting
or useful, tailoring the shopping experience to your unique
behavior. In your everyday life, you are likely utilizing AI tech-
nologies without even being aware of it. Simple tasks, such as
setting reminders, navigating through maps, or filtering out
spam emails, often depend on advanced AI algorithms working
behind the scenes. Gaining a deeper understanding of these
technologies can significantly enhance your comfort and confi-
dence in using them. You might want to consider exploring
various voice-controlled devices or apps that can help simplify


===== PAGE 7 =====
and streamline your daily tasks, ultimately making your life
more convenient and enjoyable

Different Types of AI
There are two main types of AI: Narrow AI and General AI.
Narrow AI specializes in performing a specific task, while Gen-
eral AI can handle various tasks similar to a human being. This
distinction is important because it helps us understand how AI
works in our daily lives. For example, when you use a navi-
gation app, it can guide you to your destination by processing
traffic data and giving you the best route. This is an application
of Narrow AI, as it is designed to solve one particular prob-
lem—find-ing your way. Narrow AI systems are commonly
found in smartphones, smart home devices, and customer ser-
vice chatbots.
For instance, Siri is a type of Narrow AI designed to assist
with voice commands. When you ask Siri to set an alarm or pro-
vide a weather forecast, it quickly responds to your request.
However, Siri’s abilities are limited to the tasks it has been pro-
grammed to handle. It cannot carry out activities beyond its set
capabilities, such as understanding an emotional conversation
or doing complex tasks at the same time. This is why it is clas-
sified as Narrow AI rather than General AI.
Understanding these differences can help you appreciate
the technology you interact with daily. When using a voice
assistant or any specific AI tool, remember that it is designed to
be very good at a single task, not to think or behave like a


===== PAGE 8 =====
human across a wide range of activities. The more you learn
about AI, the better prepared you will be to embrace future in-
novations that can enrich your life.

How AI Works
Think of this as a very smart friend who remembers your fa-
vorite things. When you watch movies, for example, the AI
looks at the genre, actors, and themes you enjoy. It collects this
information to figure out what you might like for your next
movie night. The more you watch, the better it becomes at
making suggestions, just like how a friend would learn your
tastes over time.
If you love heartwarming dramas, after watching a few, the
AI will pop up with titles that fit this category. It’ll say, You
might also like this movie, and often, it’s spot on. This system
works by taking what it knows about your past choices and
comparing them to a vast library of films, finding similar ones
just waiting for you to discover.
Understanding how AI runs on data helps you see its value in
daily experiences. For instance, whenever you visit an online
store that recommends items based on your shopping history,
that’s AI in action. It uses everything it knows about your pre-
vious purchases to suggest what you might want to buy next.
With AI learning from each interaction, the suggestions be-
come more personalized, making shopping more enjoyable.
Remember, the more feedback and interactions you provide
through your choices, the smarter these systems get!

Fraud Detection Systems
Banks now use artificial intelligence (AI) to detect unusual
transaction patterns that may indicate fraud. For example, if
you usually shop at your local grocery store but one day there’s

action location, amount, and frequency. If something doesn’t
seem right, it flags that transaction for further review. This
process happens quickly, often in real-time, helping to protect
your money.
If the AI notices something that looks suspicious, it can im-

email from your bank asking if you made a large purchase that

whether the transaction is legitimate or if it’s fraudulent. Such

take out loans or make other charges in your name. Having in-
stant notifications allows you to act quickly, such as freezing
your account or disputing charges before any real damage is

Protecting Personal Information
It is very important to set strong passwords and avoid shar-

actions can significantly enhance your data privacy and secu-

Recognizing Scams and Security Threats
Phishing is a trick that people use to steal your personal
information. A common example is receiving an email that
looks like it’s from your bank, asking you to click a link and ver-
ify your account. The link often leads to a fake website that
looks real, and entering your details can give scammers access
to your finances. Look closely at the sender’s email address;
sometimes, it’s slightly misspelled or uses unusual domain
names. If you receive a phone call from someone asking for
your information, remember that no legitimate company will
ask for personal details over the phone. Hang up and call the
number on their official website if you’re unsure. Trust your in-
stincts. If something feels off, it probably is.
Scammers often target older adults because they may be
less familiar with technology and more trusting. One typical
scam involves a phone call from someone claiming to be from a
health organization, offering fake health services. They may ask
for your Medicare number, saying it’s necessary for a new card
or benefits. It’s important to know that official organizations


===== PAGE 56 =====
will never ask for sensitive information in that way. Another
prevalent scam to be aware of is the lottery scam, where you
receive a letter or call saying you’ve won money but need to
pay fees to claim it. Remember, you cannot win a lottery you
didn’t enter. Keeping up to date on the latest scams can help
you stay vigilant.
A practical tip to protect yourself is to regularly monitor your
bank statements. Checking your account for unauthorized
transactions can help catch thieves before they do too much
damage. Consider discussing any suspicious communications
with family or friends, as they might offer helpful advice and
support. Always remember that it’s okay to ask questions and
take your time before responding to calls or emails seeking
personal information.
